HVAC Duct Sizing Guidelines

Proper duct sizing is critical for HVAC system efficiency, energy consumption, and occupant comfort. Our calculator uses industry-standard formulas to help you determine optimal duct diameters based on velocity and friction constraints.

Velocity Guidelines

  • Supply ducts: 500-1500 ft/min
  • Return ducts: 500-1000 ft/min
  • Main ducts: 1000-2000 ft/min
  • High-velocity systems: 2000-4000 ft/min

Friction Guidelines

  • Low-pressure systems: 0.05-0.08 in./100ft
  • Medium-pressure: 0.08-0.12 in./100ft
  • High-pressure: 0.12-0.15 in./100ft
  • Maximum recommended: 0.15 in./100ft

Best Practices

  • Consider noise levels in occupied spaces
  • Account for system pressure drops
  • Plan for future capacity requirements
  • Optimize for energy efficiency

Engineering Calculations

This calculator implements the following industry-standard formulas:

CFM Calculation (Velocity Method)

CFM = Area × Velocity

Where Area = π × (diameter/2)² in square feet

Duct Diameter (Friction Method)

D = ((CFM × 0.109136) / friction)^(1/5.02)

Based on ASHRAE friction loss calculations for round ducts
